About Page



Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is a contagious disease caused by a newly found virus in China in 2019, a part of a lethal virus family that includes SARS (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome) and MERS (Middle East Respiratory Syndrome). The SARS was detected in Southern China in 2003, and MERS was seen in Saudi Arabia in 2012. Coronaviruses are a group of related RNA viruses that cause diseases in mammals and birds. In humans, they cause respiratory tract infections that can range from mild to deadly. SARS and MERS attack the respiratory system, but Covid-19 attacks other essential organs, such as the kidneys, liver, and lungs.
